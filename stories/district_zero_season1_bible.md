# District Zero - Season 1 Bible

## Core Hook

District Zero is the last protected district of a drowned megacity. It sells security, order, and survival. In truth, it survives by sacrificing the lower population and burying the existence of life beyond the wall.

## Series Engine

Each episode pushes two tracks forward:

- the conspiracy track: who built the system, who profits from it, what exists beyond the wall
- the rebellion track: how Nara evolves from maintenance runner to strategic threat

## Commercial Positioning

- premium contained sci-fi thriller
- strong global hook
- scalable with controlled locations
- high tension without requiring constant VFX spectacle

## Season 1 Arc

Episode 1 - The Girl Who Opened the Gate
Nara discovers the hidden gate, sees proof of life beyond the wall, and learns District Zero is poisoning its own lower sectors.

Episode 2 - Passive Watch
Security lets Nara run to expose her network. Mira tests whether Nara is useful or suicidal.

Episode 3 - Flow Control
Nara uncovers how water, medicine, and power are used as tools of obedience.

Episode 4 - The Upper Light
Nara infiltrates the upper levels and sees how comfort is manufactured through selective sacrifice.

Episode 5 - Broadcast Window
The underground attempts its first public leak. Vale turns the leak into a justification for emergency powers.

Episode 6 - The Outside Signal
First direct contact with survivors beyond the wall. The outside world is real, but not simple and not uniformly safe.

Episode 7 - Fault Line
Elian's past role in the system becomes public, breaking trust inside the rebellion.

Episode 8 - Containment
Vale reveals that District Zero was never designed only to keep danger out. It was also built to keep a population in.

Episode 9 - Breach Protocol
Nara's network prepares a coordinated breach of infrastructure, communications, and the wall itself.

Episode 10 - District Zero
Season finale. The district fractures. The truth reaches the public. Nara forces open the city, but what enters next may be as dangerous as what ruled before.

## Main Character Arc

Nara begins as a survivor with technical skill and moral instinct. She becomes a witness, then a fugitive, then a leader. Her core arc is learning that exposing truth is not enough; she must also decide what kind of order replaces the old one.

## Antagonist Arc

Commander Sarin Vale is not a cartoon tyrant. He believes collapse is always one step away and that controlled cruelty is the price of civilization. His conflict with Nara is ideological as much as tactical.

## Tone Rules

- always grounded in physical systems: gates, pumps, power, water, movement, access
- fear comes from control and surveillance, not only action scenes
- emotional scenes stay tight and unsentimental
- reveals should escalate from local injustice to civilizational lie
