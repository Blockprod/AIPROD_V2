Marcus ran quickly through the narrow market streets. He noticed the morning vendors opening their stalls. He felt nervous about the letter in his coat pocket. The crowds moved around him as he pushed forward.

Later that morning, inside the old stone library, Clara sat at a long wooden table. She looked carefully at the ancient maps spread before her. She thought about the message Marcus had sent the night before. A candle flickered at her side.

"I found the passage," Clara said quietly, tracing a line with her finger.

Thomas entered the room and closed the heavy door behind him. He walked to the table and leaned over the maps. He watched Clara's hand move across the parchment.

"Are you certain?" Thomas asked, his voice low.

"The markings match exactly," Clara replied. She stood and handed him the letter.

The next day, at the old harbor docks, Marcus arrived at dawn. He moved to the edge of the pier and stared across the grey water. He wondered whether the ship had already left. Seagulls called overhead and ropes creaked against the wooden posts.

Sofia appeared from behind a stack of crates. She ran toward Marcus and grabbed his arm.

"The captain waited," she said. "We still have time."

Marcus felt relieved. He looked at Sofia and gave a slow nod. Together they walked quickly along the dock toward the waiting vessel.

Hours later, inside the ship cabin, all four gathered around a small table. Clara spread the map again and held it flat with both hands. Thomas stood by the porthole and looked out at the receding coastline. Marcus sat across from Sofia and studied her face.

"Once we reach the island," Clara said, "there is no turning back."

Sofia nodded and said nothing. She felt the weight of the decision settle over the room. The ship pitched gently and the lantern above them swayed.

Marcus stood and moved to the porthole beside Thomas. He watched the dark water churning below the hull. The coast had disappeared entirely. He thought about everything they had left behind.

"We go forward," Marcus said.
